THE RICHTER SCALE

Understanding how we measure the power beneath our feet.

The Richter scale measures the magnitude of an earthquake—the total energy released at its source. It is a logarithmic scale, meaning the numbers grow much faster than they appear.

The +1 Jump: Every whole number increase (e.g., 6.0 to 7.0) means the ground shakes 10 times harder and releases about 32 times more energy.

1–3
Very Weak: Usually not felt by people, but recorded by instruments.
4-6
Moderate: Felt by everyone; small objects might fall or break.
7–9+
Major: Massive "big ones" capable of severe damage to cities.
